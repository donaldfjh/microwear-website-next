# Meta Ray-Ban Smart Glasses Blocked in EU: How AI Glasses Manufacturers Can Tackle Compliance Challenges

> **⚡ Key Takeaway:** In 2024, Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses faced EU sales restrictions due to new battery regulations. For AI glasses manufacturers, **compliance is no longer optional—it's a hard threshold for market access**.

---

## 🚨 1. Event Review: Why Was Meta Ray-Ban Blocked in the EU?

### The Background Story

In 2024, the EU officially implemented the new Battery Regulation **EU 2023/1542**, replacing the previous Battery Directive (2006/66/EC). The new regulation introduces a revolutionary requirement:

> **🔑 All portable batteries must be designed to be replaceable by the user.**

Meta Ray-Ban smart glasses, with their non-removable built-in battery, failed to meet this core requirement.

### ⚠️ Compliance Dilemmas Faced

**Non-replaceable battery** — Integrated sealed design prevents user replacement

**Repairability not compliant** — Fails EU "Right to Repair" framework

**Environmental responsibility** — Cannot meet battery recycling requirements

### 📊 Scope of Impact

This incident extends far beyond Meta:

- 🔥 **Industry shakeup** — Most smart glasses and wearables use sealed battery designs
- 💰 **Soaring compliance costs** — Manufacturers must redesign product architecture
- 🚧 **Higher market barriers** — Non-compliant products cannot enter the EU-27 market

---

## 📋 2. EU Battery Regulation (EU 2023/1542): 5 Key Requirements

### Quick Reference

**1️⃣ Battery Replaceability** — Users replace batteries without tools — Deadline: **2027**

**2️⃣ Repairability Design** — Meet repairability scoring standards — Deadline: **2024+**

**3️⃣ Battery Passport** — Digital identity for each battery — Deadline: **Feb 2027**

**4️⃣ Carbon Footprint** — Disclose production emissions — Deadline: **Jul 2025**

**5️⃣ Recycled Materials** — Minimum % of recycled content — Deadline: **2028**

---

### 🔍 Deep Dive: What This Means for Smart Glasses

#### 1️⃣ Battery Replaceability (Most Critical)

**The Requirement:**
- ✅ Users must replace batteries **without professional tools**
- ✅ Replacement process must be simple and safe
- ✅ Spare parts supplied for 10+ years after discontinuation

**The Challenge:**
> Smart glasses pursue slim design—traditional sealed designs struggle to meet this requirement.

**Design Solutions:**

💡 **Modular battery design** → Independently removable module

💡 **Standardized interface** → Universal battery connection

💡 **Official repair services** → Compliant after-sales network

💡 **Software updates** → Extend product lifecycle

---

#### 2️⃣ Repairability Design

**What's Required:**
- Products must achieve minimum repairability scores
- Design for disassembly
- Availability of spare parts

**Timeline:** Phased implementation from 2024

---

#### 3️⃣ Battery Passport

**What's Required:**
- Digital identity for each battery
- Full lifecycle information tracking
- QR code or digital access

**Timeline:** February 2027 (industrial & EV batteries first)

---

#### 4️⃣ Carbon Footprint Declaration

**What's Required:**
- Disclose battery production emissions
- Third-party verification
- Public database access

**Timeline:** From July 2025

---

#### 5️⃣ Recycled Material Content

**What's Required:**
- Minimum percentage of recycled materials in batteries
- Gradually increasing requirements

**Timeline:** From 2028
